Heart Rate App

Where there is a problem, there should be an app.

What is it about?

With devices like the Apple Watch you can measure your heart rate pretty easily. All you need is a watch and a heart rate and off you go. But as soon as you want more than your current heart rate or a list with the raw data points, the health app provided by Apple can’t help you anymore. But I wanted more. Good thing I married an iOS developer.

The Goals

At the end I wanted an app that shows me the last measured heart rate, gives me a daily, weekly and monthly overview and lets me comment on each measurement, this way I can see what I have done at the point of measurement, e.g. working out, relaxing, arguing with someone or stroking a dog.

The Approach

There aren't a lot of apps out there that provide what I was looking for. Therefore the research part for this project was very slim. And because we already knew what we wanted in the app, we were able to start with the framework right away.

The Framework

The idea was to build an app that contains everything necessary but nothing more. Easy and simple. So we reduced everything to our main goals: See last measurement, monthly, weekly and daily overview and being able to comment.

The Mockups

For the Mockups we stayed within our “easy and simple” credo. We eliminated everything unnecessary and made everything else visible, discoverable and understandable. No need for explanations to understand what you have to do to use the app. Sounds easy and desirable but is actually hard work. You have to think a lot about each and every thing you put on the screen and ask yourself, if it is necessary or if there is an easier way.

The Colors

After everything has its place and purpose you can start making it pretty. In my opinion, the looks are as important as the functions for a product to be successful. Without the necessary functions you can’t use the product properly, but without it looking appealing, you don’t want to use it. Both parts can be a big problem. So why not make it pretty? We started with a nice color palette. I think it is important to reduce your palette to a maximum of 5 colors. This way you can alternate and differentiate but don’t start looking like you took too many drugs while working on the colors. Decide on one basic color for the background and built on that.

The Outcome

Besides the iPhone App we decided on building an Apple Watch App. This way you can comment on a measurement right after it. Just click the app and enter your current activity.

What's next?

While working on a project, you always have ideas what else would be nice to have. Our first version of the app is the simplest way. Basic features which make it easy to use and understand the app. Next up, there could be some more possibilities like being able to export your recorded data, change colors or our own icons to have a quick and fun way to comment on your measurements.
We are excited to see what will come.
